Pickleball Rules
🏓
Scoring (Rally Scoring)
Every rally scores a point, regardless of who served. First to reach target (11, 15, or 21) wins — must win by 2. At 10-10 (for 11pt), play continues until a 2-point lead is gained.
🎯
The Serve
Must be underhand (paddle below wrist). Ball struck below waist. Serve diagonally cross-court, landing in opposite service box. Server must keep both feet behind the baseline. Only one serve attempt per rally.
🏠
The Kitchen (Non-Volley Zone — NVZ)
The 7-foot zone on each side of the net. Players CANNOT volley (hit the ball in the air) while standing in the kitchen. You may step in to play a bounced ball, but must exit before volleying. This is the most common fault.
⬇️
Double-Bounce Rule
After the serve, the receiving team MUST let the ball bounce once before returning. The serving team must also let the return bounce before volleying. After these two bounces, both teams may volley freely.
⚠️
Common Faults
• Ball lands out of bounds
• Ball hits the net
• Kitchen fault (volleying from NVZ)
• Serve fault (wrong court or out)
• Ball bounces twice before return
🎮
Doubles Rules
Both players on a team serve before the serve passes to the other team (side-out). Exception: at the very start, only one player serves. Server must call score (server score, receiver score, server number) before each serve.

Chess Guide
♟
Pieces & Values
Pawn=1 · Knight=3 · Bishop=3 · Rook=5 · Queen=9. The King is invaluable — losing it ends the game.
♛
Objective
Checkmate your opponent's King — place it under attack with no escape. Draws occur by stalemate, 50-move rule, threefold repetition, or insufficient material.
⚡
Time Controls
• Bullet (1-2 min): Ultra-fast instinctive play
• Blitz (3-5 min): Fast, popular online format
• Rapid (10-30 min): Balanced, most common
• Classical (60+ min): Deep strategic play
📝
Algebraic Notation
e4=pawn to e4 · Nf3=knight to f3 · O-O=kingside castle · O-O-O=queenside castle · Qxd5=queen captures d5 · e8=Q=pawn promotes to queen · +=check · #=checkmate
🖱️
Interactive Board
Tap any piece to select it — legal moves appear as green dots. Tap a dot to move. Use the Material Balance section to track captures and advantage.

Quick Start Guide
New to Arena? Here's how to get up and running in minutes.
① Choose a sport from the home screen — Cricket, Football, or Badminton.
② Go to Match → Create or Join a room. Share the room code with friends.
③ Set up your teams or players, then start the match.
④ Open the Live tab to score in real time. Friends on the same room see it instantly.
⑤ History tab shows all past matches with scorecards and gallery.
Room Codes Explained
• The Admin (room creator) has full edit access.
• Viewers can watch live scores but cannot change them.
• Share the code or link for others to join as viewers.
• Admin can promote a viewer to Editor for co-scoring.
